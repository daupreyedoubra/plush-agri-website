# Product

## Register

brand

## Users

**Primary:** Smallholder and semi-commercial livestock farmers across Northern Nigeria — experienced hands who know their land but lack reliable access to credentialed veterinary guidance. They access information through WhatsApp, community networks, and field visits. Many are Hausa-speaking. They aren't looking for a flashy website; they're looking for a trustworthy professional who will come to them.

**Secondary:** Commercial livestock operators needing structured herd health management and reliable input supply. Development partners (NGOs, government agencies, international programmes) evaluating PAS as a field-credentialed implementing partner. Farmer cooperatives seeking training and bulk input access.

**Context of use:** A development partner opens this site on a laptop to evaluate PAS before a partnership meeting. A semi-commercial farmer or their representative lands here via Instagram or WhatsApp referral. Both need to feel immediately that this is a serious, credentialed organisation — not a charity, not a corporate vendor.

## Product Purpose

Plush Agri Solutions closes the last-mile gap between credentialed veterinary expertise and the farmers who need it across Northern Nigeria. The website's job is to establish institutional credibility with development partners and stakeholders while remaining legible and warm for farming communities. Success is a development partner who books a call, a farmer who saves the phone number, or an NGO that initiates a partnership inquiry.

## Brand Personality

**Three words:** Grounded. Credible. Present.

Voice is direct — precision without filler. Expert — authority felt through field specificity, not title-first credentials. Warm — the farmer is a capable professional who deserves the organisation's best information. Field-honest — no claim that hasn't been tested in real conditions.

Emotional goal: Confidence. The visitor should feel this organisation knows exactly what it's doing and will show up. Not reassurance from above — assurance from beside.

## Anti-references

- **Generic agri-NGO sites** — UN/FAO institutional aesthetic, teal-and-orange charity palettes, stock photos of smiling farmers holding tablets in temperate green fields, heavy development-speak copy. PAS is a professional advisory firm, not a charity.
- **Multinational agribusiness** — Bayer, Syngenta, Corteva. Clinical, Western, product-catalogue feel, white-lab-coat imagery, corporate distance. PAS comes to the farm; it doesn't wait at a counter.
- **Saturated editorial-typographic lanes** — display serif italics, mono labels, broadsheet grids. The brief is not magazine-shaped.

## Design Principles

1. **Come to the ground.** The website mirrors the organisation's field-first model: information is direct and rooted in real conditions, not abstracted into institutional language. Every design decision should feel like a practitioner, not a marketer.

2. **Authority through specificity.** Credibility is built by naming the problem exactly — the disease, the season, the region — not by listing credentials first. Show, then tell.

3. **Bilingual by design.** Hausa copy is not a translation footnote — it is a signal that this brand is rooted in the community it serves. Treat it as a typographic element with its own voice and placement.

4. **Imagery as field evidence.** Photography must reflect real Northern Nigerian farming conditions — the light, the landscape, the people. Clinical, temperate, or generic African-continent stock imagery reads as dishonest.

5. **Palette as authority.** The deep Field Authority green (#1F4D36) and Veterinary Trust blue (#163A70) carry institutional weight. These colours signal stability and expertise. Don't hedge them with excessive softness — let the palette speak.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

WCAG 2.1 AA. Bilingual English/Hausa throughout. Legible on mid-range Android devices (primary access for farming audience via WhatsApp referrals). Reduced motion support via `prefers-reduced-motion` media query. Colour contrast minimum 4.5:1 for body text, 3:1 for large text.
